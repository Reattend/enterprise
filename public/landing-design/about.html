About Reattend

We build the memory companies forget they need.

Reattend started in 2023 with a stubborn observation: the most expensive thing inside any company isn't compute, headcount, or even time — it's the institutional knowledge that walks out the door every quarter. We're a team of researchers, infrastructure engineers, and former CISOs building the system of record for enterprise memory.

What we believe.

Three convictions sit underneath every product decision we make. We don't always get them right — but we always come back to them.

Knowledge compounds — or it leaks.

Every meeting, ticket, and design call you don't capture is a future bug, a re‑asked question, a re‑built feature. Memory is the cheapest leverage in the company.

Trust is a system, not a slogan.

An AI that handles your most sensitive context has to log every recall, gate every retrieval, and never train on what isn't yours. We treat compliance as a product surface.

Recall is the unit of value.

Indexing is table stakes. The hard part — and the part nobody else gets right — is returning the one paragraph that actually answers the question, with citations a lawyer can defend.

A short history.

From four people in a Bengaluru WeWork to enterprise deployments on three continents, in roughly chronological order.

Mar 2023

The founding bet.

Rohan, Priya, Daniel, and Aisha lock themselves in a WeWork in Indiranagar with a whiteboard and one question: "Why does enterprise AI keep re‑asking the same questions?"

Aug 2023

First $4M seed · Sequoia leads.

The seed round closes on a 14‑page memo and a working prototype that ingests Slack and answers a question with a citation. The prototype is held together with duct tape; the memo, less so.

Feb 2024

Design partner #1 ships.

An 800‑person fintech replaces three internal wikis with Reattend. Their head of engineering tells us, "I didn't know how much we were forgetting until we stopped." We take that line and never give it back.

Sep 2024

Rabbit‑LLM v1 trained in‑house.

We move off third‑party recall models. Latency drops 4×. Retrieval precision goes from 71% to 94%. The team takes one week off, badly.

Apr 2025

SOC 2 Type II · clean report.

Zero critical findings. Two minor observations, both closed in two weeks. CISOs start calling us instead of the other way around.

Nov 2025

$32M Series B · Lightspeed leads.

340 enterprises on the platform. We open the New York office and start the work to support EU‑only and India‑only deployments end‑to‑end.

Mar 2026

ISO 27001 + India region.

ap‑south‑1 goes live with full DPDP alignment. ISO 27001 stage 2 audit passes. We celebrate with terrible cake and excellent dosa.
